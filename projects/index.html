Japanese Language Processing Thesis

Education through Data

Current technologies that estimate the pronunciation of a Kanji employ the help of look-up dictionaries. This is great for getting high accuracy, however the models are unable to be parsed into a human readable rule book to help learners of the language gain an insight on the features that influence which pronunciation is used in context. For my research, I am using the (Kanjidic2 dataset for individual Kanji pronunciations, JMdict dataset for the vocabulary words, ENAMDICT dataset for proper nouns, and MeCab for parsing text. Click here to view my current progress.

Work to be done:

  • Split up the pronunciations of words and align parts of the pronunciations to the Kanji/Kana
  • Determine important features and their relationships
    • Pronunciation of syllable before and after Kanji of interest
    • Kanji/Kana type of grapheme before and after Kanji of interest
    • Meaning of vocabulary
  • Train the Bayesian Network
